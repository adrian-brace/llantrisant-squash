SQUASH

RULES

In your box there will be approximately 6 players (including yourself).

The goal is to play as many players as you can (once) in your box within the cycle Start and End dates. Cycles will be approximately 6 weeks in length depending on the time of year.

Best of 5 games. Point A Rally to 15.

Scores are entered on Squash Levels after the match. Enter results as either game totals or each points total in each game (see Squash Levels section).

Once scores are entered, you are automatically awarded points. You will be rewarded for playing your ladder games at 2 points per appearance.

The league points system is as follows:

  • If you win 3-0, your ladder points are 7-2
  • If you win 3-1, your ladder points are 6-3
  • If you win 3-2, your ladder points are 5-4
  • If you lose 3-0, your ladder points are 2-7
  • If you lose 3-1, your ladder points are 3-6
  • If you lose 3-2, your ladder points are 4-5
  • If you run out of time then players are asked to replay the tie before the end of the ladder or agree that the current leader is granted the win with inflated scores entered that appropirately reflect the state of play when you ran out of time.

Promotion/Relegation

  • 1st and 2nd in each box get promoted 1 division.
  • Bottom and 2nd bottom in each box get relegated 1 division.

Squash Levels

Your match scores will feed into the global Squash Levels system and contribute to global ranking points. In the case where an experienced player is playing an inexperienced player, we suggest entering just the games total, not individual points per game. This provides the opportunity for the experienced player to make a game of it, instead of going for the maximum possible margin of win to save their ranking points.

  • 1st and 2nd in each box get promoted 1 division.

Late joiners

  • New joiners to the ladder can be added up to 10 days into a cycle. After that they will be added to the subsequent cycle.
  • The organiser reserves the right to start a joiner higher up than the bottom to avoid drastic mismatches in abaility, and to cater for returns from injuries.

Walkovers

This is a friendly ladder designed simply to:

  • Encourage participation against people of a similar level
  • Meet new people
  • Develop your game and learn from each other in a safe and mildly competitive environment
  • Bridge people into participating in league squash

Please use walkovers ONLY as a final resort. We would discourage ever using them but here is guidance as a final rule when there's a disagreement:

  • No show on the day without contact.
  • Cancelled on you twice in a cycle within 24 hours of match time.
  • Failure to respond to your messages/calls within 10 days of first trying.
  • Opponent injured for 3 or more weeks of the cycle.
  • Opponent on holiday for 2 or more weeks within the cycle.
  • Failure to agree a time to play after 3 times of trying (providing first attempt to offer was at least 2 weeks prior to the end of the cycle) e.g. You can't just offer 3 times in last week and take a walkover.

The 2 participation points gained from entering a walkover will be discounted from final standings except if a walkover happens on the court (e.g. injury whilst playing the ladder match).

In most of the above scenarios we would hope everyone tries to be understanding of any extenuating personal circumstances that may have occurred which caused a problem trying to fulfill a fixture, and still be open to rearranging the game without taking a walkover.

A walkover requires the Injury Icon to be selected upon points or games entry in Squash Levels. I believe this stops it going onto Squash Levels and affecting ranking points.

NOTES

  • Box size is at organiser's discretion.
  • The organiser reserves the right to start a joiner higher up than the bottom to avoid drastic mismatches in abaility, and to cater for returns from injuries.
  • The ladder administrator reserves the right to make subtle changes depending on lack of participation over numerous cycles and the level of any new joiners. The organiser will always act to be fair but without creating big mismatches.
  • Failure to play any fixtures over a successive period of ladder cycles can result in you being removed from the ladder entirely.
